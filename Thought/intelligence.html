chapter 01

a neuron is a tiny opinion

Strip away the mystique and a neuron is a few multiplications. Each input gets a weight — how much this neuron cares about it. Add a bias — its general mood — and squash the total between 0 and 1. That's the whole organism.

Drag the sliders. Watch the wires thicken as weights grow, flip color when they go negative, and watch the output cell brighten as the opinion strengthens. Learning, when it comes, will simply be the art of turning these knobs automatically.

chapter 02

sixty-four pattern hunters

Your drawing arrives as a 28×28 grid of brightness — 784 numbers. They feed 64 hidden neurons, which feed 10 outputs, one per digit. Loudest output wins.

Here's the beautiful part. Nobody told the hidden neurons what to hunt for. chapter 03

learning is falling, carefully

Training is golf in 50,890 dimensions. The loss measures how wrong the network is; every weight asks "if I nudge myself a little, does the wrongness shrink?" — and then all 50,890 of them step downhill together. Calculus finds the slope. Repetition does everything else.

Below, one lonely weight rolls down its own loss bowl, leaving a trail. The learning rate is the stride length — push it past 0.9 and watch the poor thing overshoot the valley and ricochet between the walls.

chapter 05

the whole secret, in twenty lines

Everything above scales. The network in this page was trained with exactly this loop — guess, measure the wrongness, feel for downhill, step. Repeated 840,000 times. The largest models on Earth are this loop with more zeros attached.

import torch, torch.nn as nn
from torchvision import datasets, transforms

train  = datasets.MNIST('data', train=True, download=True,
                        transform=transforms.ToTensor())
loader = torch.utils.data.DataLoader(train, batch_size=32, shuffle=True)

model = nn.Sequential(
    nn.Flatten(),
    nn.Linear(28 * 28, 64),   # 784 pixels -> 64 pattern hunters
    nn.ReLU(),
    nn.Linear(64, 10),        # 64 hunters -> 10 verdicts
)

opt     = torch.optim.SGD(model.parameters(), lr=0.05)
loss_fn = nn.CrossEntropyLoss()

for epoch in range(14):
    for x, y in loader:
        opt.zero_grad()
        loss = loss_fn(model(x), y)   # how wrong are we?
        loss.backward()               # which way is downhill?
        opt.step()                    # take the step

A pile of weighted opinions, a way to measure wrongness, and the patience to fall downhill 840,000 times.
You just taught one by hand.
